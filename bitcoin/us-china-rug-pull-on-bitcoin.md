## The Thesis

> What if China buying Iranian oil with Bitcoin is the rug pull — executed jointly by China and the US? Oil holds value. The US dollar holds value when the AI bubble bursts. Then governments and Wall Street dump Bitcoin and ban it.

The claim: three events trigger simultaneously — the AI bubble bursts, Bitcoin gets mass-dumped by state actors and institutional holders, and global regulatory bans land all at once. The result is a permanent kill shot on Bitcoin, timed to benefit dollar hegemony and oil-denominated wealth.

---

## The Case Against

### 1. The Coordination Problem Is Enormous

China and the US are in active economic warfare — conflicting interests on Iran, Taiwan, Saudi Arabia, and global reserve currency dominance. These governments can't agree on steel tariffs. A covert multi-year financial sting operation requires a level of trust between peer adversaries that has no historical precedent.

### 2. China Has No Incentive to Kill Bitcoin

China has been quietly accumulating Bitcoin as a hedge against dollar-denominated sanctions risk — exactly what they watched Russia suffer in 2022. Crashing Bitcoin destroys an off-ramp they spent years building. Their rational play is to use Bitcoin, not bury it.

### 3. The US Can Already Ban Bitcoin Without This Scheme

The US has far simpler tools: exchange regulation, capital controls, tax enforcement, stablecoin legislation. The SEC and CFTC already have enormous suppressive power they haven't fully used. A decade-long geopolitical theatre is unnecessary.

### 4. Oil and Dollar Correlation Doesn't Work the Way You Need

Oil is priced in dollars. A deflationary crash typically strengthens the dollar **and** crushes oil demand simultaneously. The 2008 crash saw oil drop from $147 to $35. The hedge collapses exactly when you need it.

### 5. Bitcoin's Decentralization Makes a "Rug Pull" Mechanically Hard

The US government holds ~200k BTC — under 1% of supply. A full dump is a bad week for Bitcoin, not an extinction event. On-chain visibility means sophisticated holders would front-run it before it happened.

### 6. Iran Is the Weak Link That Breaks the Logic

Iran needs dollar-equivalent purchasing power for imports. If they accept Bitcoin knowing it's about to crash, they're the mark in this scheme. Their central bank has navigated sanctions for 40 years — they're not that naive.

## Steel-Manning the Thesis

### On #2 — China's Incentive

China's public ban on private crypto coexists with controlling 55%+ of Bitcoin's hash rate. They've built a large government Bitcoin position through seizures. An entity holding Bitcoin while publicly banning it and selectively liquidating it looks exactly like a player positioning for a coordinated dump.

### On #3 — The Three Bombs Thesis

The mechanism: coordinate Wall Street, US Treasury, Chinese state actors, and mining infrastructure to time a simultaneous sell, ban, and AI bubble burst. Mining control handles network leverage. Price action triggers the cascade. Regulatory coordination makes the ban stick.

> *When you hold enough of an asset, you can most certainly sell it all at once and move markets down in a big way. This is market mechanics — not fragile at all.*

Germany dumping 46,000 BTC caused a 15% drop. Scale that to US + China + BlackRock + Strategy selling simultaneously and you have a genuine cascade event.

### On #4 — Game Theory of Dollar Strength + Oil Drop

A stronger dollar hurts emerging market debt holders. A coordinated oil price drop kneecaps Russia, Venezuela, and Iran simultaneously. The game theory logic is coherent.

Who benefits from a stronger Bitcoin and weaker dollar? All US Treasury holders and countries trying to avoid fiat collapse. Who benefits from a stronger dollar and weaker Bitcoin? Everyone running dollar-denominated systems. Ask who benefits — the answer points in a coherent direction.

### On #5 — The Coordinated Dump

The who-sells list:

- US government (~198k BTC)
- China (~194k BTC)
- BlackRock ETF redemptions forced by a market crash
- MicroStrategy/Strategy margin calls
- Bitcoin-backed loan liquidations cascading

This is a real waterfall scenario. Concentrated holders can and do move markets.

### On #6 — Iran-China Yuan + Bitcoin Oil Trade

Iran is routing oil export revenue through yuan and Bitcoin explicitly to avoid the US dollar. China accepts Bitcoin for oil, accumulates, and can then dump. Iran's goal is dollar bypass, not Bitcoin preservation — they don't need to care if Bitcoin crashes after the trade settles.

## Evidence and Concessions

### Conceding #2 Fully

Despite a public ban on private crypto ownership, Chinese mining pools controlled **over 55% of Bitcoin's hash rate** as of 2024. China quietly built a position as the world's second-largest Bitcoin holder, with at least **194,000 BTC**. A government-affiliated think tank at Renmin University called Bitcoin a strategic reserve asset in May 2025.

Beijing has pursued limited and selective liquidation of these holdings — exactly what a player positioning for a coordinated dump would look like.

### Major Concession on #6 — More Right Than Wrong

This is happening now, not as speculation:

- China buys **over 80% of Iran's oil exports** at discounted rates, with payments increasingly in yuan to reduce US oversight and advance renminbi internationalization *(Al Jazeera)*
- Iran is requiring ships passing through the Strait of Hormuz to pay **cryptocurrency — specifically Bitcoin** — ensuring payments can't be traced or confiscated under sanctions *(The Hill)*
- Iran's National Security Committee approved a bill formalizing yuan and stablecoin tolls, accelerating dollar bypass in energy trade *(Bitcoin News)*

The de-dollarization infrastructure is being built in real time, with Bitcoin and yuan as the explicit alternative rails.
